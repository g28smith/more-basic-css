Web Design Best Practices

Planning Your Website

Before you start building, take time to plan your content structure and visual design.

Always sketch your layout on paper first. This saves time and helps you think through the user experience.

Writing Good Content

Content should be clear, concise, and easy to read. Break up long paragraphs and use headings to organize information.

Keep paragraphs under five sentences when possible. This makes text easier to scan on screens.

Remember that users often skim rather than read every word.

Choosing Colors

Color choices affect both aesthetics and accessibility. High contrast helps users read your content.

Test your color combinations to ensure they meet accessibility standards for color contrast.

Typography Matters

Font selection impacts readability. Choose fonts that are clear at various sizes.

Sans-serif fonts like Arial work well for headings. Serif fonts like Georgia are good for body text.

Limit yourself to two or three fonts maximum. Too many fonts make your site look unprofessional.

Testing Your Site

Always test your website in multiple browsers and on different devices before launching.

Mobile users now make up more than half of web traffic. Your site must work well on phones and tablets.
